The claw (duck head) </h3> <br></br> I designed the joint in fusion as one piece that can be printed and functions straight after support material is removed. It contains an inner shaft and a circular part that contains the shaft and lets it rotate on the inside. Both of these have an extrusion that form the two parts of the claw. <br></br> There are also raised areas on these extrusions to hold a rubber band. This allows the claw to have two positions, open & flexed, or loose & closed. All effort will go into opening the claw, and no effort will go into closing it, making the mechanism simpler, and reducing the need for a separate motor function/boundary (making sure the claw doesn't close "too far" and strip the gears in the motor or break the part) <br></br> I also included a little support system I made out of zip ties to create an area for the string to go. <br></br> <img src="./IMG_8822.jpeg" width = "400"> <img src="./IMG_8823.jpeg" width = "400"> <br></br> This was the first version that I printed to make sure it would actually move: <br></br> <video width="640" height="480" controls> <source src="IMG_8824.mov" type="video/mp4"> </video> <br></br> This is the second version that I printed with long attachments to connect to the electronics housing and bring it further out as to not interfere with my hand. <br></br> *occasionally this still does interfere with my hand, and I am considering raising the grabbing aspect to around a 45 degree angle above my hand, with the idea that I can hold 3 things at once, or perhaps 4 if I have a contraption for each hand.* (This is a very simple change and can be accommplished easily by just tilting the angle in which the joint connects to the housing.) <br></br> I also added two rubber stickers to add to the grippiness of my duck-grabber's head: <br></br> <img src="./IMG_8844.jpeg" width = "400"> <br></br> An essential part fell off of the 3D print, and I was able to graft it back onto the print using the soldering iron, which I was proud of.
